# Lesson: AI for Email Writing

## Introduction and context

Email is still one of the main communication channels in work and personal life.

Most email problems come from unclear structure, weak asks, and inconsistent tone.

AI helps by giving you a fast first draft that you can refine.

## Why AI is useful for this topic

AI can help you:
- draft clear subject lines
- summarize long points into short messages
- adjust tone for different audiences
- turn rough notes into a send ready draft

Example prompt:

Write a concise status update email to my manager. Include progress, one blocker, and the next step.

## How to ask for better help on this topic

Strong prompts include audience, intent, and constraints.

Weak prompt:
Write an email about my project.

Better prompt:
Write a professional email to a client explaining we need two extra days. Keep it under 150 words. Offer a revised timeline and ask for confirmation.

## Practical examples

1. Follow up email after no reply in one week.
2. Meeting request with two time options.
3. Update email with completed tasks and open risks.
4. Thank you email after an interview.

## Try this yourself

1. Ask AI to draft an email you need to send today.
2. Ask AI to rewrite it in a warmer tone.
3. Ask AI to shorten it by 30 percent without losing key details.

## What to watch out for

1. Sending without review.
2. Using generic tone that does not sound like you.
3. Missing key facts like dates, owners, or next actions.

## Check your understanding

### Question 1
What three details should you include in an email writing prompt?

Answer: Audience, intent, and constraints like length or tone.

### Question 2
Why should you review AI generated email drafts before sending?

Answer: To catch context mistakes, tone mismatches, and missing facts.

### Question 3
What is one way AI helps reduce email writing time?

Answer: It can turn rough notes into a structured first draft.
