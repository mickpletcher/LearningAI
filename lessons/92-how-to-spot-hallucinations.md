# Lesson: How to Spot Hallucinations

## Introduction and context

As AI tools become common, the real advantage is not using AI more often. It is using AI more wisely.

This means checking quality, understanding limits, and applying judgment before taking action.

Strong workflows reduce errors, protect sensitive information, and improve confidence.

## Why AI is useful for this topic

AI is useful here because it helps with identifying confident sounding but incorrect AI content.

It can quickly structure ideas and surface options, but your review is what makes the result reliable.

Example prompt:

Help me with How to Spot Hallucinations. Keep it practical, explain risks, and provide a step by step approach.

## How to ask for better help on this topic

Weak prompt:
Did AI hallucinate here?

Better prompt:
Review this output for hallucination risks and identify unsupported claims, fabricated details, and confidence cues.

A stronger prompt includes context, constraints, and what success should look like.

## Practical examples

1. Detect unsupported specifics in an answer.

Example prompt:
Mark statements that look precise but have no evidence or citations.

1. Identify suspicious citation patterns.

Example prompt:
Review these cited references and flag anything that looks fabricated or unverifiable.

1. Rewrite uncertain sections responsibly.

Example prompt:
Rewrite this response to clearly label uncertainty and avoid unsupported claims.

1. Create a hallucination review checklist.

Example prompt:
Create a quick checklist to detect likely hallucinations before using AI output.

## Try this yourself

1. Pick one real task and apply this lesson method to it.
2. Improve your prompt with context, constraints, and quality checks.
3. Review and validate the output before using it in real decisions.

## What to watch out for

1. Trusting confident answers without verification.
2. Sharing sensitive details that should stay private.
3. Skipping review because the output sounds polished.
