# Lesson: Building a Team AI Workflow

## Introduction and context

As AI tools become common, the real advantage is not using AI more often. It is using AI more wisely.

This means checking quality, understanding limits, and applying judgment before taking action.

Strong workflows reduce errors, protect sensitive information, and improve confidence.

## Why AI is useful for this topic

AI is useful here because it helps with aligning team standards so AI use is consistent and safe.

It can quickly structure ideas and surface options, but your review is what makes the result reliable.

Example prompt:

Help me with Building a Team AI Workflow. Keep it practical, explain risks, and provide a step by step approach.

## How to ask for better help on this topic

Weak prompt:
Our team should use AI more.

Better prompt:
Help us design a team AI workflow with role responsibilities, quality checks, and governance rules.

A stronger prompt includes context, constraints, and what success should look like.

## Practical examples

1. Define team roles in AI workflow.

Example prompt:
Define roles and responsibilities for AI usage in this team process.

1. Standardize prompts and review steps.

Example prompt:
Create a shared prompt and review standard for this team task.

1. Create a team quality and safety checklist.

Example prompt:
Build a checklist for quality, privacy, and compliance checks in team AI use.

1. Plan team adoption milestones.

Example prompt:
Create a 30 day adoption plan for this team AI workflow with milestones.

## Try this yourself

1. Pick one real task and apply this lesson method to it.
2. Improve your prompt with context, constraints, and quality checks.
3. Review and validate the output before using it in real decisions.

## What to watch out for

1. Trusting confident answers without verification.
2. Sharing sensitive details that should stay private.
3. Skipping review because the output sounds polished.
