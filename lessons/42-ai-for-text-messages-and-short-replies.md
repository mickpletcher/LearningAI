# Lesson: AI for Text Messages and Short Replies

## Introduction and context

Text and chat replies are common in daily life and work.

People often overthink short responses or send messages that are unclear.

AI helps you generate quick reply options with better tone.

## Why AI is useful for this topic

AI can help you:
- write concise replies quickly
- adjust tone from casual to professional
- avoid awkward wording
- offer multiple options to choose from

Example prompt:

Write three short reply options to confirm a meeting. One friendly, one neutral, one formal.

## How to ask for better help on this topic

Include these inputs:
- who you are replying to
- what you want to say
- desired tone
- length limit

Weak prompt:
Reply to this text.

Better prompt:
Write a polite one sentence reply to my coworker confirming I can meet at 2 PM.

## Practical examples

1. Confirming a plan.
2. Politely declining an invitation.
3. Asking for clarification in one sentence.
4. Following up after delayed response.

## Try this yourself

1. Paste a real message and ask AI for two reply options.
2. Ask AI to shorten your reply to one sentence.
3. Ask AI to make a draft more respectful without changing meaning.

## What to watch out for

1. Replies that sound robotic.
2. Missing context in very short messages.
3. Tone that does not match the relationship.

## Check your understanding

### Question 1
What information improves short reply prompts?

Answer: Audience, intent, tone, and length.

### Question 2
Why is tone control important in short messages?

Answer: Short messages can be misread quickly without context.

### Question 3
What is one practical way to use AI here?

Answer: Ask for multiple reply options and choose the best fit.
