# Lesson: Using AI to Brainstorm Ideas

## Introduction and context

Brainstorming is one of the easiest ways to get immediate value from AI.

You can use it when you need names, topics, project ideas, marketing angles, gift ideas, writing angles, solutions to a problem, or just more possible directions than you can come up with alone.

AI is useful here because it can generate many possibilities quickly. That does not mean all the ideas will be great. It means you get a wider starting pool to react to.

## Why AI is useful for brainstorming

AI helps brainstorming because it is fast, tireless, and willing to suggest options without worrying about whether they are perfect.

It can help you:
- generate many ideas quickly
- offer variations on a theme
- suggest categories or directions
- combine ideas in unusual ways
- break a blank page problem
- help you react to options instead of inventing everything from scratch

Often the value is not that AI gives you the final answer. The value is that it gives you enough material to trigger your own better ideas.

### Example

`Give me 20 possible names for a beginner friendly AI newsletter. Make half sound professional and half sound more approachable.`

That is a strong brainstorming prompt because it asks for volume and variation.

## How to brainstorm better with AI

A few habits make brainstorming much more useful.

### 1. Give the topic clearly

Say what kind of ideas you want.

### 2. Add constraints

Say things like:
- beginner friendly
- low budget
- professional
- fun
- practical
- under 30 minutes
- for a small business

### 3. Ask for range

Tell AI to give you different styles or categories.

### 4. Ask for more after the first round

The first list is usually not enough. Ask for:
- more unusual ideas
- cheaper ideas
- more serious ideas
- ideas closer to a certain audience

### Example

Weak prompt:  
`Give me ideas.`

Better prompt:  
`Give me 15 low cost weekend project ideas for a homeowner who likes practical DIY work. Mix indoor and outdoor ideas.`

## Practical examples

### Example 1

Content ideas:

`Give me 25 beginner friendly blog post ideas about using AI in everyday life.`

### Example 2

Problem solving:

`Give me 10 practical ways to reduce clutter in a small home office.`

### Example 3

Naming ideas:

`Give me 20 names for a productivity app. Keep them short and easy to remember.`

### Example 4

Event ideas:

`Give me 15 low stress birthday party ideas for a family gathering with mixed ages.`

## Try this yourself

### Exercise 1

Pick one real area where you feel stuck and ask AI for 15 ideas with a clear constraint.

### Exercise 2

Take the first list and ask for a second round that is more specific, cheaper, faster, or more creative.

### Exercise 3

Choose the three strongest ideas and ask AI to expand each one into a short plan.

## What to watch out for

### 1. Asking for ideas with no constraints

That usually leads to bland lists.

### 2. Treating all ideas as equal

Brainstorming is for generating options, not for pretending every option is good.

### 3. Stopping after the first batch

The second and third rounds are often where the good ideas start.

## Check your understanding

### Question 1

What are three ways AI helps with brainstorming?

**Answer:**  
Examples include generating many ideas quickly, offering variation, combining angles, breaking through blank page problems, and expanding rough ideas.

### Question 2

Why are constraints helpful in brainstorming prompts?

**Answer:**  
Because they make the ideas more relevant, practical, and tailored to the real situation.

### Question 3

What is one good next step after getting an initial brainstorm list?

**Answer:**  
Ask for a second round with tighter constraints, more variation, or expansion of the strongest ideas.
