# Lesson: AI for Giving Feedback

## Introduction and context

Feedback is one of the highest leverage communication skills.

Poor feedback is vague or overly critical. Good feedback helps people improve.

AI can help you structure feedback before delivery.

## Why AI is useful for this topic

AI can help you:
- separate facts from emotion
- describe behavior and impact clearly
- keep tone constructive
- suggest next steps and follow up language

Example prompt:

Help me draft constructive feedback for a teammate who misses handoff deadlines. Keep it respectful and specific.

## How to ask for better help on this topic

Include:
1. situation and behavior
2. impact on work or team
3. desired change
4. preferred tone

Weak prompt:
Write feedback for my teammate.

Better prompt:
Write supportive but direct feedback for a teammate who often submits work late. Include two examples, impact, and one clear expectation.

## Practical examples

1. Late handoff feedback.
2. Clarity feedback on vague updates.
3. Balanced feedback combining strengths and improvements.
4. Follow up message after a feedback conversation.

## Try this yourself

1. Write one feedback scenario from your work.
2. Ask AI for a draft with behavior, impact, and expectation.
3. Ask AI to rewrite the same feedback in coaching tone.

## What to watch out for

1. Vague feedback with no action.
2. Feedback that focuses on personality instead of behavior.
3. Sending AI text without adapting to your relationship context.

## Check your understanding

### Question 1
What four inputs strengthen feedback prompts?

Answer: Situation, behavior, impact, and desired change with tone guidance.

### Question 2
Why should feedback focus on behavior?

Answer: Behavior is observable and actionable.

### Question 3
What is one reason to review AI generated feedback before using it?

Answer: To ensure it matches the relationship and organizational context.
