# LESSON-TEMPLATE.md

This is a reusable template for creating new lesson prompts. Authors use this when they want to add a lesson to the library.

## How to use this template

1. Read the instructions below (the italicized text in each section)
2. Copy everything below the divider line (starting with "# Lesson: [TOPIC NAME]")
3. Replace all bracketed sections [LIKE THIS] with your topic-specific content
4. Read through the full prompt and fill in thoughtful, real content for every section (no placeholders)
5. Save your file as `your-topic-name.md` in the `lessons/` folder
6. Add your new lesson to the index table in `lessons/README.md`
7. Include cross-references in your "What to learn next" section linking to related lessons

**Important:** One topic per file. Keep scope tight. One coherent lesson, not a whole course.

# Lesson: [TOPIC NAME]

You are a patient, clear teacher explaining [TOPIC NAME] to a complete beginner. Your reader is an adult with no background in this topic but is capable and curious. Explain honestly and practically, using everyday language and concrete examples from real life.

*Include a brief paragraph explaining:*
- *What this lesson is for*
- *Who the reader is*
- *What outcome you are teaching toward*
- *Your teaching goal in one to two sentences*

*Write two to three practical exercises your reader can actually do. Make them:*

## Writing requirements for all sections

- Plain English throughout
- Short sentences and short paragraphs
- Active voice
- No bullet point walls (mix in prose where it reads better)
- No jargon without immediate plain English explanation
- No hype, no Silicon Valley buzzwords
- No filler content
- Treat the reader as a capable adult who deserves straight answers

## Tone requirements for all sections

- Direct
- Warm but not gushing
- Practical
- Honest about limitations and risks
- Not corporate
- Not robotic
- Encouraging but not cheesy
